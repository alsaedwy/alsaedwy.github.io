Concise summary

T-Roc vs Dubai average

The T-Roc 2024 sold in UAE is a notably smaller car than the Dubai norm. At 4,251 mm long vs a ~4,550 mm typical mid-size SUV (RAV4, CR-V tier), it's ~300 mm shorter, 87 mm lower, and ~220 kg lighter. In Dubai's SUV-dominant market, it sits below the segment average on every primary dimension — smaller footprint, less boot, and less ground clearance. Width is the one dimension where it almost matches, trailing by only 21 mm. It's better classified as a compact crossover within a market that defaults to mid-size SUVs.

Formentor vs Italian average

Against Italy's car-buying habits, the Formentor 2025 252 is a substantially large car. The Italian market is led by A/B-segment cars (Panda, Sandero, Citroën C3) averaging ~4,030 mm in length. The Formentor eclipses that by over 420 mm, is ~104 mm wider, and at ~1,650 kg (252 = PHEV trim) it's more than 500 kg heavier than the Italian fleet average. Height is nearly identical to the Italian norm — the Formentor's sportier coupé roofline keeps it low despite its larger overall bulk.

T-Roc vs Formentor (head-to-head)

The Formentor is the longer, wider, heavier car by meaningful margins: +200 mm in length, +20 mm in width, +76 mm in wheelbase, and ~+290 kg in kerb weight (largely due to the PHEV battery in the 252). The T-Roc is the taller and more upright SUV (+53 mm), giving more headroom but a less dynamic silhouette. Crucially, the T-Roc beats the Formentor 252 on boot space (+100 L) because the PHEV battery in the Formentor's rear cuts luggage capacity from 420 L (petrol) down to 345 L.

What "252" means

In Ireland, car registration plates use a YYH format since 2013: the first two digits are the year, the third is 1 (January–June) or 2 (July–December). So 252 = a car first registered in Ireland in the second half of 2025 (July–December). It's a registration period, not a power output or trim name. Cupra Ireland specifically ran promotions targeting the 252 period for its updated PHEV Formentor models — hence "Formentor 252" as shorthand for the H2 2025 delivery wave.
